How to Identify Phishing Emails

Best Practice 1

Verify the Sender

Always check the sender's email address carefully before taking any action. Phishing emails often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but contain subtle differences.

What to look for:

  • Misspellings in the domain name
  • Extra characters or numbers
  • Different domain extensions (.co instead of .com)
  • Suspicious sender names
Legitimate: networksupport@bestpointgh.com

Suspicious: networksupport@bestpoint-gh.com
support@bestpointgh.co
networksupport@bestpointgh.com.secure.net
Best Practice 2

Recognize Warning Signs

Phishing emails typically share common characteristics that can help you identify them.

Common red flags:

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Requests for passwords or personal information
  • Generic greetings ("Dear User" or "Dear Customer")
  • Poor grammar or spelling errors
  • Unexpected attachments
  • Links that don't match the displayed text
Warning signs: "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours"

"Click here to verify your credentials"

"Unusual activity detected - respond immediately"
Best Practice 3

Report Suspicious Emails

If you receive an email that seems suspicious, do not click any links or open attachments. Instead, follow these steps:

  • Do not reply to the email
  • Do not click any links or download attachments
  • Forward the email to our IT team
  • Delete the email from your inbox
  • If in doubt, contact the sender through official channels

Remember: It is better to report a false alarm than to ignore a real threat. Our IT team would rather investigate 100 safe emails than miss one genuine attack.

Real Phishing Examples

Below are actual examples of phishing emails that have been used to target organizations. Study these carefully to recognize similar attacks.

Example 1: Fake IT Department Email

Microsoft 365 Account Alert

From: IT Support <support@bestpoint-gh.com>

Subject: Urgent: Your Office 365 Account Has Been Compromised

Dear Kwame,

Your Office 365 account has been compromised.

We have blocked your account for your own safety. Someone in Lagos, Nigeria attempted to log into your account kwame.mensah@bestpointgh.com several times.

Please follow these steps below to sign in and keep your account safe:

  1. Protect your account [LINK]
  2. Learn how to make your account more secure [LINK]

Thanks,
Microsoft Office 365 Team

Red Flags:
  • Sender domain is bestpoint-gh.com not bestpointgh.com
  • Creates urgency with "compromised" and "blocked"
  • Microsoft wouldn't send from our domain
  • Generic sign-off from "Microsoft Office 365 Team"
Example 2: Fake Bank of Ghana Alert

Wire Transfer Cancellation Notice

From: Bank of Ghana Alerts <alerts@bankofghana-gov.com>

Subject: Wire Transfer Cancelled - Action Required

Hello Akua,

This is an alert notice:

A wire transfer recently initiated from your Best Point Savings and Loans account (by you or another person) was cancelled by the Bank of Ghana Payment Systems yesterday.

Amount: GH₵ 25,000.00
Reference Number: BOG-2024-8847261

Please click [HERE] to view further information and verify your account details.

-Kofi Mensah, Operations Team Lead
Bank of Ghana Payment Systems

Red Flags:
  • Sender uses bankofghana-gov.com (fake domain)
  • Bank of Ghana wouldn't contact you directly about our internal transfers
  • Creates panic with large amount and cancellation
  • Requests you to "verify account details"
Example 3: Fake Company Announcement

Internal Company News

From: Communications Team <news@bestpointgh.com>

Subject: Breaking News - What's Happening at Best Point

This email is being sent to all employees with a company email address.

Breaking News

We are very proud to talk about what has been happening at Best Point Savings and Loans. There is so much going on to be proud of across our branches in Accra, Kumasi, and Takoradi.

But what makes this email more important aside from showing you what is going on in other departments is discussing how this will affect you.

We want to make sure that no employee feels left out because we value you Ama.

I'm sure you're eager to hear about what's going on at our company, so go ahead and click the link below.

[EXCITING NEWS LINK]

Best Regards,
The News Team

Red Flags:
  • Vague subject matter with no specific details
  • Unusual sender "The News Team" - not a real department
  • Builds curiosity to make you click
  • No official Best Point branding or formatting
Example 4: Fake Mobile Money Scam

MTN Mobile Money Alert

From: MTN MoMo <alerts@mtn-momo.com>

Subject: Your MoMo Account - Urgent Verification Required

Dear Kwabena,

We recorded a payment request from your MTN Mobile Money account to enable the charge of GH₵ 500.00.

Transaction ID: #MTN7841062P
Merchant: Online Shopping Ghana
Status: PENDING

Because the order was made from a foreign IP address, we put an Exception Payment on this transaction motivated by our Security Tracking System.

THE PAYMENT IS PENDING FOR THE MOMENT.

If you didn't make this payment and would like to decline the GH₵ 500.00 billing, please follow the link below to cancel the payment:

[CANCEL THIS PAYMENT]

NOTE: Because email is not a secure form of communication, please do not reply to this email.

Copyright © MTN Ghana 2024

Red Flags:
  • MTN uses SMS for MoMo alerts, not email
  • Suspicious domain mtn-momo.com (not official MTN)
  • Creates panic with unauthorized charge
  • Poor grammar: "to enable the charge"
Example 5: Fake OneDrive Share

Document Shared Via OneDrive

From: Yaw Boateng <yboateng@bestpointgh.com>

Subject: Urgent: Q4 Financial Report - Review Required

Hello Abena,

Please refer to the vital financial documents I've shared with you using OneDrive.

Click [https://onedrive-secure.com/docs/bestpoint-q4] and sign in with your Best Point credentials to view details.

This requires your immediate attention for the board meeting tomorrow.

Regards,
Yaw

CONFIDENTIALITY NOTICE: This e-mail and any transmitted files are private and confidential. Please consider the environment before printing this e-mail.

Red Flags:
  • URL is onedrive-secure.com not microsoft.com
  • Asks you to "sign in with Best Point credentials"
  • Creates urgency with "board meeting tomorrow"
  • OneDrive shares come from sharepoint.com or 1drv.ms
Example 6: Fake Ghana Revenue Authority

Tax Compliance Notice

From: GRA Compliance <compliance@gra-gov.gh>

Subject: Outstanding Tax Payment - Final Notice

Dear Taxpayer,

Our records indicate that Best Point Savings and Loans has an outstanding tax liability of GH₵ 45,000.00 for the 2023 fiscal year.

Reference Number: GRA-2024-5847
Due Date: 15th December 2024
Penalty for Late Payment: 25% surcharge

To avoid legal action and additional penalties, please review your tax statement and make payment immediately by clicking the link below:

[VIEW TAX STATEMENT AND PAY NOW]

For enquiries, contact us at 0302-XXX-XXX

Ghana Revenue Authority
Tax Compliance Division

Red Flags:
  • GRA would send official mail, not direct payment links
  • Suspicious domain with hyphen gra-gov.gh
  • Threatens legal action to create panic
  • Our finance department handles all GRA communications
Example 7: Fake Outlook Storage Limit

Microsoft 365 Storage Alert

From: Microsoft 365 Admin <admin@bestpointgh.com>

Subject: Action Required: Your Mailbox Has Exceeded Storage Limit

Hello,

The IT Department was running an Office 365 server migration last night and encountered an error with several email accounts.

Your account kofi.asante@bestpointgh.com happened to be one of them.

Your email kofi.asante@bestpointgh.com has exceeded one or more size limits set by the Network Admins. We attempted to resolve this problem and believe we have fixed it.

Due to various emails received by our Microsoft 365 users of receiving spam, Best Point will be upgrading email system to 100GB of space.

If your email has been working just fine, please let us know by going to this secure [WEBSITE LINK] and validate your email address is working.

Click [HERE] to login to your account for confirmation and upgrade.

Your account will remain active after you have successfully upgraded to the new 100GB storage.

Thanks for your quick cooperation,
IT Department
Best Point Savings and Loans

Red Flags:
  • IT would use networksupport@bestpointgh.com, not admin@bestpointgh.com
  • Multiple contradictory reasons (migration error + storage limit + spam + upgrade)
  • Microsoft 365 manages storage automatically - no manual "upgrade" needed
  • Legitimate IT notifications come through our Microsoft 365 admin center
  • Asks you to "validate" by clicking external link
  • Poor grammar: "We attempted to resolve this problem and believe we have fixed it" - contradicts asking for action
